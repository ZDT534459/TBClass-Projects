And now, it's time to rate!

Biggums bbq

So to start us off, I'm going with BBQ, mainly because of how often I crave it.
Biggums BBQ happens to be a family favorite for me, especially due to it being such a rare treat.
Each year I go up into Ellijay with my grandparents to purchase apples and other delicious treats from a particular
orchard store, and when lunch comes around my folks always seem to find their way to biggums.
My personal favorite there, are the ribs. A full rack with a few extra sides is more than enough to fill all three of us up.

Rating between a 4/5 (Mainly due to how long and cramped the lines are!)

Cookout

Whenever I happen to be in the mood for a burger, and find myself with some extra pocket change, you can count
on Cookout to be a great place to visit for a fullfilling burger/fries/side/and drink, all for around 4-5$!
My personal favorite there aside from the burgers of course, are their corndogs, nothing taste better than a mustard slathered corndog!
Just like at the georgia fairgrounds!

Between a 4/5 (Mainly due to them not having my favorite watermelon flavored milkshake year round!)

Hibachi Shop

If you're in the mood for a filling piece of tai, or just looking for something flavorfull, I recommend this place.
It has a wide variety of goods, ranging from just plain udon noodles with stir fried rice, to my personal favorite, takoyaki!
A yummy mushy ball of bread, beef, and chewy squid in the center!
All complimented with a tasty combo of thousand island sauce and mayo along the top.

Personal rating, a 5/5 (Mainly due to it being the first time I had Takoyaki!)

Marietta Fish Market

This place is a personal family favorite as well, going as our #1 choice for birthday dinners.
Especially since they offer you a free slice of New York Styled Cheesecake for free on your b-day!
My favorite dish has to be the Salmon crab patties, slathered in white sauce and complimented with a mixed
variety of seafood chowder on the side. Never in my life have I ever tasted such a new flavor
and the ambiance is quite lovely to admire as well with the giant fish tank taking up the spotlight as well.

Between a 4/5 (Mainly because their booths are a bit hard for me to get in and out of due to the grooves blocking us)

Doucher de France

Now comes my favorite part, the dessert! Fish market can only offer you cake, while this place offers you
a straight up sweet tooth buffet! Beignets, macadamia cookies, creamhorns, you name it, they probably have it.
One of my favorite things to buy there are the Macarons. I simply LOVE macarons. Such a sweet crunchy shell of thin
proportions, all squished together around a tastey filling. I cannot think of a better snack/dessert
than a Macha green tea macaron on a spring afternoon in the woods.
Truely an experience of inner peace to behold.

A pure 5/5!
